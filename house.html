1

How much
is it worth?

Get a quote or two, a second opinion never hurt anyone. But, never lie to the first about what the second told you, or vice versa. Once you have a reasonable agent and offer that you’re happy with, it’s time get the ball rolling.

2

Instructing your agent

Without an agent, your property can’t be listed on sites like Rightmove and Zoopla, and then you have to make the choice of either an online agent or an in person one. With an online agent, there’s no guarantee that you will deal with the same person each time you need help. But with Hunters, there’s always a friendly, human voice that you will come to recognise during the process of selling your property.

3

Best foot forward

First impressions matter. Take an objective look at your house, do you need to repaint to neutral colours? What little and large things need fixing? Now’s the time to do all the jobs you’d been putting off. Upgrading your kitchen and adding a bathroom can add to the value of your home, but you’d be amazed what adding lamps and brightening up a room can do as well.

4

Choosing your
legal best friend

- AKA a solicitor

Some things you can do without a solicitor, selling your home isn't one of them. The legal process of transferring property ownership is called conveyancing, and although your solicitor won’t technically get involved until you’ve accepted an offer, you’ll want them ready to go as soon as you have.

5

Accepting an offer

After people have come to view your wonderful home and have started making offers, it’s time to get accept one, and kick everything into high gear.

6

Laying the groundwork

- negotiating the draft contract

Figure out what fixtures and fittings will be included and how this will affect your agreed price. And don’t forget, you also need to agree how long a time period you’ll have between exchanging contracts and moving out.

7

All the lists and questionnaires

The buyer’s lawyer will need to ask everyone some questions before final contracts are exchanged. Your questionnaire will revolve around things like fixtures and fittings included in the sale, the boundaries of the property, and information about any disputes or complaints with your neighbours.

8

The point
of no return

- exchanging contracts

When all the terms are finally agreed, final contracts will be written up and exchanged. Everyone is now legally committed to the sale. Now, if you pull out without just cause, you can be sued and the buyer will have their deposit returned to them. Equally, if the buyer pulls out now, they’ll lose their deposit.

9

Moving out

It’s time to find those boxes, hire a van or hijack some generous friends, and buy take out and/or other bribes for the people you rope in to help you move.

10

Completion

- the last big step

The property is officially changing hands now, you’ll accept the payment, say goodbye to your keys and try not to shed a tear. The bulk of the money will be transferred by each party’s respective solicitors along with the deed, and your solicitor will register the transfer of ownership with the land registry.

11

Pay off your
mortgage

By the time completion is occurring your mortgage provider will have given you your exact redemption figure. If you’re changing mortgage supplier then there is going to be an early exit fee, if you’re staying with the same company it varies from provider to provider. Now that the majority of the money is with your solicitor, you can have them pay off your mortgage for you! … Well, this one at least.

12

The last bills

Just this one last thing and then you can relax, your solicitor needs paying for their services. They will send you an account of all their costs, the sale price and the redemption of the mortgage.

And finally, rest.
